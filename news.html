Amazon Road


Brazil are going to pave a road that goes straight through the Amazon rainforest. Researches say that this road will cause further deforestation to the Amazon, destroying the ecosystem. The paved road will help with connecting Brazils areas however, deforestation will increase dramaticly as its easier to get machines to the rainforest.

This area is one of the Amazons most untouched areas, with the introduction into better road quality the area will not be as untouched as trees will need to get cleared for the road to get paved. This has been a summary of the sky article (Sky.2022).

Amazon Degragation


The guardian has reported that a third of the Amazon rainforest is being degraded by droughts and human causes. Droughts are causing water shortages across the rainforest making the trees more fammable and prone to forest fires. Furthermore, it stops new plants from growing which would replenish biodiversity

Human causes like logging is destroying the Amazon increasingly reducing the capacity to store carbon. This needs to slow down as the guardian states that 17% of the rainforest has been lost in the past century.

This information has been a summary from (Guardian.2023)

Goods Ban


BBC has reported that goods that are a product of deforestation will not get sold in the EU. It includes products that include palm oil and some furniture that was created by logging. This is a significant development has it would reduce the demand for products that require deforestation therefore, reducing the amount of carbon dioxide being released. There will to checks to mke sure that companies selling to the EU is following this regulation.

This has been a summary from (BBC, 2022)

COP26


BBC reported that world leaders want to end deforestation by 2030 which comes with over 14 billion pounds of funds to meet this objective. Some countries will also stop the import of palm oil that come from deforestation. BBC has stated that the countries pledged has 85% of a forest cover in the world.

Even through forest loss is slowly reducing there is still a overall lost of cover which will continue. Industries are driven by the demand for beef and timber so the pledge if followed will stop the destruction of all forests including the Amazon.
