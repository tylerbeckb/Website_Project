What is deforestation?


Deforestation is the activity of cutting down trees and clearing forests purposefully (National Geographic.2023). The main driver is to create an area for agriculture or to collect wood as a resource. Deforstation is one of the main contributers of climate change as stored carbon is released into the atmosphere. For example:


  • • Wood for fuel
  • • Palm oil plantation
  • • Animal grazing














Causes of Deforestation


There exists both human and natural causes for deforestation however, human actions are increasing the frequency of natural causes.


1. Forest Fires


Forest fires is a natural cause of deforestation, they can vary in size from small ones that burn a few hundred trees, to a global problem that can burn a whole rainforest. They're becoming increassingly frequent as more hot summers and more serve droughts, are making the perfect conditions for a fire to happen.


2. Animal Grazing


This is a major human cause that is responsible for 14% of global deforestation . As the population grows the demand for food increases too. This causes farmers to want more fertile land for animal grazing and to grow crops for the animals. A forest is perfect for animals to graze as there is fertile soil and they can reuse to wood cut down as fuel. The population of cattle as rising dramaticly and deforestation has increased therefore, this is a major human cause that needs to be stopped.




Impact of Deforestation


Deforestation causes imense impacts to world with accelerating climate change massively. Trees take in carbon dioxide and store it for many years but the increasingly act of cutting down these trees will release this stored up carbon in the atmosphere which increases the amount of greenhouse gases in theh atmosphere therefore, increasing the enhanced greenhouse effect.


1. Loss of Biodiversity


The forest accomodates multiple different species and biodiversity with the destructions of trees these habitats are loss. This causes a reduction of biodiversity as there is less food and this will create a multiplier effect that will reduce all animals in the foodchain (youmatter.2020).


2. Desertification


Deforestation causes more soil to be exposed to direct sunlight, this will dry up the soil and produce cracks. When rain falls the soil won't be able to absorb hardly any water as the rain can't infiltrate the hard soil. This will cause desertification as the land slowly begins to die and makes it hard to grow any plants.
