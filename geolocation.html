Sumatra Rainforest

Sumatra rainforest is one of the most rapidly deforested places on the planet due to illegal logging and not enough funding to help stop it. Indonesia contribute over a 3rd of global carbon emissions from deforestation. Palm oil plantations are being fuelled by demand which contributes heavily to Indonesian deforestation. 50 million people live in the forest and rely on it to survive. However, with the shift to destroying the forest many are straving or forced to leave their home as nature gets burnt around them.

Amazon Rainforest

The Amazon is the largest rainforest in the world with it having over a 10th of all known species in the world. It is threaten by illegal resource overextraction, for example, logging and over fishing. These are unsustainable and will cause the collapse of the ecosystem. A major threat is forest fires, with it releasing tons of CO2, it increases the intensisty of future fires. These are major threats that are affecting the people and biodiveristy Over 40 million people rely on the amazon and so does the world to prevent a climate crisis.
