Plant Trees


Help by either planting or donating money to a charity that will plant a tree for you. This will help as the tree you planted will absorb CO2 to produce oxygen. It is the most simple but effective way that you can do it home. You can make it a community activity, then the whole community can plant a tree which would make a real difference.

There are mulitple charities that you cann plant a tree with like the national trust. To donate search 'plant a tree' into google to press the button below to go straight to the national trust donation page.

Reduce Meat Intake


The consumption of significant amount of meat fuels the act of deforestation as cattle ranching is one of the main drivers as animals like cows need a lot of space. Global demand keeps increasing causing more forests being cut down for space.

You don't have to stop eating meat entirely however, reducing meat intake by half or even a third will help reduce the demand for meat if more and more people take this opportunity.



Recycle


The act of recycling everyday makes a big impact and creates many benefits. Recycling wood and paper will reduce the demand therefore reducing the amount of logging taking place. (The World Counts.2023)

Buying recycled products will also produce the same effect and it also helps support those companies and charities fighting to save deforestation. It reduces the amount of waste going to landfills. There are labels on products that informs you if they have been reycled so next time you got shopping look out for the sticker.



Educate People


Educating people about the impacts of deforestation will help raise awareness of this major issue. This will increase the chance action will take place due too as an increasingly amount of people will be putting pressure on governments to act.

Education can create a multiplier effect where if you educate one person they might educate another which will get more people to act.





Don't Buy Palm Oil Products


Palm oil has become a significant cause of degradgation of rainforests as areas are being cleared for plantations. 17% of Malaysia forests were lost between 2002-2020 due to palm oil plantations (Bloomberg UK. 2021). As palm oil demand keeps increasing, deforestation will keep taking place.

Palm oil is in loads of products for example: chocolate; soap; ice cream; shampoo and many more (WWF.2023). We need to raise awareness and you can help my looking for palm oil free logo on the back of products so you can make sure you're not contributing to the destruction of trees.
